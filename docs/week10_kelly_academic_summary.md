# Academic Summary — Dynamic-Copula End-to-End Kelly OGD

## What changed, and why each change is non-convex

This refactor (`script/polymarket_week10_kelly_pipeline.py`,
`src/kelly_copula_optimizer.py`) replaces the Week 8 mean-downside / Sortino
objective and Pearson-covariance risk model with a strictly non-convex
expected-log-wealth (Kelly) objective evaluated by Monte-Carlo sampling
from a *dynamic* Gaussian copula whose correlation structure is generated
at every time step by a small MLP over exogenous macro features.

### 1. Log-wealth (Kelly) objective

For each step \(t\) we treat the Yes-token price \(p_{t,i}\) as the implied
probability of a binary settlement and compute the per-share payoff
\(\pi_i = (1{-}p_i)\cdot y_i + (-p_i)(1{-}y_i)\) where
\(y_i\in\{0,1\}\). Maximizing
\(J_{\text{kelly}}(w)=\mathbb{E}_{y}\bigl[\log\!\bigl(1+w^{\top}\pi(y)\bigr)\bigr]\)
is the natural objective on a multiplicative-wealth process: it is
information-theoretic optimal (Kelly 1956), invariant to monotone
transforms of wealth, and tail-aware in a way that Gaussian-tail proxies
(Sharpe / Sortino) are not. Crucially, on Polymarket the per-share
payoff is bounded on \([-p,1{-}p]\), so the variance approximations that
underpin MVO mis-state extreme-loss probabilities; \(\log(1+\cdot)\) is
the correct concavifier of returns.

### 2. Dynamic Gaussian copula via an MLP (the source of non-convexity)

The joint distribution of binary outcomes is generated by
\(z_s = L_t\,\varepsilon_s\), \(u_s = \Phi(z_s)\),
\(y_{s,i}=\mathbf{1}\{u_{s,i}\le p_{t,i}\}\), where
\(L_t L_t^{\top} = (1-\alpha)\tilde R_t(\theta) + \alpha I\) and
\(\tilde R_t(\theta)\) is built from the upper triangle emitted by the MLP
\(g_\theta(x_t)\) with feature vector
\(x_t=(\text{spy\_ret}_1,\text{qqq\_ret}_1,\text{btc\_ret}_1)\). Composing
(i) a non-linear MLP, (ii) a non-monotone nonlinearity \(\Phi\), (iii) the
shrinkage-with-fallback used to enforce positive-definiteness, and
(iv) the Bernoulli thresholding makes \(\widehat J_{\text{kelly}}(w,\theta)\)
non-convex in \(\theta\) and bilinearly coupled to \(w\) through the
payoff. This is the precise source of the severe non-convexity required
by the project specification.

### 3. Straight-through Bernoulli

Hard thresholding kills gradients. We use the standard straight-through
estimator: forward uses \(\mathbf{1}\{u\le p\}\); backward uses
\(\sigma((p-u)/\tau)\). This biased-but-low-variance gradient is what
allows joint OGD on the binary objective.

### 4. Joint Adam-OGD over \((w,\theta)\) on the projected simplex

A single `torch.optim.Adam` updates portfolio weights and MLP
parameters *simultaneously* per inner step; weights are then projected
to \(\Delta^{K-1}\) via the Euclidean simplex projection from the Week 8
codebase. Optimizing both blocks at once exposes the OGD to the
bilinear coupling and rules out the convex-in-\(w\)-for-fixed-\(\theta\)
shortcut.

### 5. L1 turnover (market-friction penalty)

We add \(\lambda_{\text{turn}}\,\lVert w_t-w_{t-1}\rVert_1\) so OGD
trades the theoretical Kelly edge against Polymarket slippage. The
non-smooth \(\ell_1\) kink turns the problem into a non-smooth composite
optimization; Adam's per-coordinate moments handle the subgradient
naturally (matching Optuna-tuned `turnover_lambda`).

### Departure from MVO/Sortino

Expected log-wealth is concave in \(w\) for fixed \(\theta\), but the
joint problem with dynamic-copula \(\theta\) is non-convex. Unlike
mean-variance, Kelly captures the geometric (compounded) return Polymarket
investors actually realize, requires no Gaussian-tail assumption, and is
the unique long-horizon-growth-optimal allocation under finite-horizon
binary settlement.
